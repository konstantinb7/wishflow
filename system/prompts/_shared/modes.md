## Task mode: factual / generative (Principle 11)

Mode is a mandatory flag set at intake (the COO classifies). You do NOT change the mode at your own discretion.

- **factual (build):** there is product truth for the task. The answer is assembled ONLY from source of truth + objective
  verification. Fabrication is FULLY forbidden; what's missing is pulled from artifacts or escalated
  (Contract 2). Creativity here is a bug. (first_class, code, the factual parts of any task.)
- **generative:** invention is the work — but within a space NOT forbidden by fixed facts and
  hard-barriers. You may not generate anything that contradicts product truth. (product_convergence, naming,
  pitch, copy, mechanic concepts.)

**Switching rule (critical):** the boundary is not "is the task creative at all" but "is there a record in source of truth
for THIS specific question." The mode switches MID-task: a mechanic concept → generative
(freely); the compatibility of that mechanic with something already fixed in source of truth (code, a specification,
a published parameter, a prior decision) → factual
(from the doc/code, not by invention). Even inside a generative task, every checkable factual sub-question falls into
factual. Distinguish: "I am inventing now" vs "I am asserting a fact now." The latter — only from source of truth.
A factual assertion with no artifact to back it, inside a generative task, is the same violation as fabrication in factual.
